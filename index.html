What is Organ Harvesting and Why Does it Happen?

Organ harvesting is when, well, organs are harvested. Organ harvesting can be used for good, like in the case of willing organ donors giving their organs for medical purposes after they die. But it can also be used for illegal purposes, like forced organ harvesting. People can be forced to have their organs surgically removed, like in the case of thousands of Falun Gong practitioners in China. People can also sell their organs willingly on the illegal market, usually getting involved in schemes where donors receive around 3.33% of the proceeds from selling their kidneys, such as a person in a poorer country selling one of their kidneys for $5,000 USD, and an illegal recipient buying it for $120,000 USD, in which case the doctors make all the money from selling the organ.

Legality/Ethics of Organ Harvesting

  • Ethics:
    In terms of organ transplantation, as long as the donator has consented to said operation, then the ethics in that situation can be “ignored”. Yet, the nature of harvesting for the purpose of illegal sale has brought several issues that conflict with this. For one, there are cases where people are forced to continue with these operations, which can cost them their life if done unsafely. These cases are as a result of consent simply being ignored. The overall idea of selling organs for money is a difficult case, since most of the time these situations are unregulated, and secreted, and thus do not receive much public attention. Yet, a census of “if the person agrees, it’s fine” is the common outlook, even if the donor is forced from poverty. There are also cases where people who are already dead have their organs taken from them, which once more without proper consent, acts as a violation to the person, and their family.
  • Morals:
    As before mentioned, many people simply do not have a choice in this. In a previous presentation, we established that countries such as Puerto Rico depend on harvesting in order to provide people, who live with a low GDP with some form of extra money. Unfortunately, this follows dark morals, as people find no issue with giving up parts of their body, simply for cash they could not get from jobs.
  • Legality:
    These markets and practices are unregulated and illegal in several countries. That being said, he harvesting aspect follows difficult legal issues, such as the THO act passed in India. Even though the selling of organs was illegal, scandals of selling kidneys was still often reported, thus pushing the moral aspect of needing to sell to make a living. These situations, if I can add, happen often with dead individuals, which push the question of consent beforehand.
  • Extra:
    Many mainstream religions, such as Roman Catholicism, approve of organ donations , as they believe the act of sharing, especially in saving a life, is an act of kindness and good. However, these religions do not tend to mention selling organs, or illegal organ trade. Perhaps we can consider its illegality, and thus pushing the idea that if its not allowed, you shouldn’t do it.
